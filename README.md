## Install

```bash
npx @human-avatar/skills-for-humanity
```

Run again to update. Restart Claude Code when done.

---

## Use

```
/s4h
```

Describe your situation. Routes to the right methodology automatically. You don't need to know which skill fits — that's the point.

Every skill pauses before running: states its read, confirms what it's actually analysing, and asks how deep to go. Correct the framing at any point — the analysis follows the corrected read, not the original one.

---

## Use cases

**Decisions** — `/s4h-decision` for structure, `/s4h-decision-premortem-analysis` to stress-test before committing, `/s4h-constraint-hardness-testing` to separate real obstacles from assumed ones.

**Complex situations** — `/s4h-systems` to map why something keeps producing the same outcome, `/s4h-game-theory` for the incentive structure underneath, `/s4h-historical` for the pattern it's an instance of.

**Creative work** — `/s4h-creativity-brainstorm` for multi-method exploration, `/s4h-constraint-rule-inversion` to make limits generative, `/s4h-aesthetic-elegance-testing` to find what's unnecessary.

**Communication** — `/s4h-communication-objection-mapping` to anticipate resistance, `/s4h-narrative-tension-mapping` to find what earns attention, `/s4h-writing-rhetoric` to audit what a piece is actually doing.

**Ethics** — `/s4h-ethics-check` for a fast multi-framework scan, `/s4h-ethics-council` for adversarial analysis, `/s4h-identity-values-clarification` to test against what you actually value.

**Investigation** — `/s4h-investigation-triangulation` for independent corroboration, `/s4h-investigation-source-trace` to trace a claim to its origin, `/s4h-investigation-counter-hypothesis` to pressure-test your explanation.

### Think Sharper

#### `/s4h-logic`

- [`/s4h-logic`](docs/logic/README.md) — Entry point for the logic toolkit.
- [`/s4h-logic-argument-validation`](docs/logic/argument-validation.md) — Check whether an argument's premises actually support its conclusion, and identify logical fallacies.
- [`/s4h-logic-causality-mapping`](docs/logic/causality-mapping.md) — Map causal relationships, trace dependencies, and reason about consequences before acting.
- [`/s4h-logic-check`](docs/logic/check.md) — A fast, comprehensive logic report on any argument, plan, or reasoning — validates premises, tests inference, detects fallacies, surfaces hidden assumptions, and produces a verdict.
- [`/s4h-logic-consistency-check`](docs/logic/consistency-check.md) — Surface internal contradictions, conflicting requirements, and edge cases that expose hidden conflicts in a document, spec, plan, design, or set of requirements.
- [`/s4h-logic-constraint-mapping`](docs/logic/constraint-mapping.md) — Map the full constraint landscape for a decision, design, or plan — distinguishing hard limits from soft preferences, surfacing hidden constraints, and finding conflicts between them.
- [`/s4h-logic-council`](docs/logic/council.md) — Run a reasoning problem, argument, plan, or decision through a council of 5 logical reasoning advisors who analyze it from distinct reasoning frameworks, peer-review each other, and synthesize a verdict on whether the reasoning holds.

#### `/s4h-probability`

- [`/s4h-probability`](docs/probability/README.md) — Entry point for the probability toolkit.
- [`/s4h-probability-base-rate-anchoring`](docs/probability/base-rate-anchoring.md) — Anchors estimates in historical base rates before adjusting for specific factors.
- [`/s4h-probability-confidence-calibration`](docs/probability/confidence-calibration.md) — Tests whether stated confidence levels match available evidence — catching overconfidence and underconfidence.
- [`/s4h-probability-expected-value-calculation`](docs/probability/expected-value-calculation.md) — Calculates expected value to compare options under uncertainty.
- [`/s4h-probability-scenario-weighting`](docs/probability/scenario-weighting.md) — Assigns explicit probabilities to distinct scenarios before making a decision.

#### `/s4h-decision`

- [`/s4h-decision`](docs/decision/README.md) — Entry point for the decision toolkit.
- [`/s4h-decision-criteria-weighting`](docs/decision/criteria-weighting.md) — Runs a weighted multi-criteria analysis — making explicit what matters, how much, and how each option performs against it.
- [`/s4h-decision-option-mapping`](docs/decision/option-mapping.md) — Ensures all real options are visible before choosing — countering the false dichotomy that limits consideration to the first two options that came to mind.
- [`/s4h-decision-premortem-analysis`](docs/decision/premortem-analysis.md) — Imagines the decision has been made and failed — then diagnoses why.
- [`/s4h-decision-reversibility-analysis`](docs/decision/reversibility-analysis.md) — Categorises a decision by reversibility and applies the appropriate level of process rigour.

#### `/s4h-constraint`

- [`/s4h-constraint`](docs/constraint/README.md) — Entry point for the constraint toolkit.
- [`/s4h-constraint-hardness-testing`](docs/constraint/hardness-testing.md) — Tests whether a stated constraint is real — distinguishing genuine limits from assumptions, habits, or politics dressed as facts.
- [`/s4h-constraint-rule-inversion`](docs/constraint/rule-inversion.md) — Flips a constraint into a creative driver — uses the limit as the generative force rather than working around it.
- [`/s4h-constraint-scope-reduction`](docs/constraint/scope-reduction.md) — Finds the minimum that satisfies the actual requirement — stripping everything wanted but not needed.
- [`/s4h-constraint-workaround-mapping`](docs/constraint/workaround-mapping.md) — Finds paths around a fixed constraint without removing it — routing around a hard limit to reach the same goal.

#### `/s4h-game-theory`

- [`/s4h-game-theory`](docs/game-theory/README.md) — Routes to the right game-theory skill for your strategic situation.
- [`/s4h-game-theory-auction`](docs/game-theory/auction.md) — Analyses bidding strategy and auction design — how much to bid, how to avoid the winner's curse, and how to design revenue-maximising or efficient auctions.
- [`/s4h-game-theory-coalition`](docs/game-theory/coalition.md) — Analyses which coalitions form and how to divide gains fairly using cooperative game theory and the Shapley value.
- [`/s4h-game-theory-equilibrium`](docs/game-theory/equilibrium.md) — Finds the stable outcome of a strategic interaction — the point where no player can improve their result by changing their strategy alone.
- [`/s4h-game-theory-iterated`](docs/game-theory/iterated.md) — Analyses long-run repeated interactions — how cooperation forms, how trust is built, how defection spirals start, and which strategies sustain cooperation.
- [`/s4h-game-theory-mechanism-design`](docs/game-theory/mechanism-design.md) — Designs rules and incentive systems that produce desired outcomes even when players are self-interested.
- [`/s4h-game-theory-prisoners-dilemma`](docs/game-theory/prisoners-dilemma.md) — Analyses cooperation problems where individual rationality produces collective irrationality.
- [`/s4h-game-theory-signaling`](docs/game-theory/signaling.md) — Analyses credibility problems and designs signals that are believable because they're costly to fake.

#### `/s4h-epistemology`

- [`/s4h-epistemology`](docs/epistemology/README.md) — Entry point for the epistemology toolkit.
- [`/s4h-epistemology-epistemic-status`](docs/epistemology/epistemic-status.md) — Produces an honest, rigorous calibration of what you know vs. believe vs. assume vs. hope across a domain.
- [`/s4h-epistemology-justification`](docs/epistemology/justification.md) — Analyzes what would actually justify believing a claim.
- [`/s4h-epistemology-knowledge-types`](docs/epistemology/knowledge-types.md) — Maps what kind of knowing is actually in play for a claim or question.
- [`/s4h-epistemology-limits`](docs/epistemology/limits.md) — Identifies what can't be known and why, then clarifies what can be established within those limits and reframes the question into its answerable part.

#### `/s4h-investigation`

- [`/s4h-investigation`](docs/investigation/README.md) — Entry point for the investigation toolkit.
- [`/s4h-investigation-claim-decomposition`](docs/investigation/claim-decomposition.md) — Breaks a complex claim into its smallest independently verifiable parts, classifies each sub-claim, and identifies which parts carry the most logical load.
- [`/s4h-investigation-counter-hypothesis`](docs/investigation/counter-hypothesis.md) — Generates the best alternative explanations for the same observations.
- [`/s4h-investigation-evidence-audit`](docs/investigation/evidence-audit.md) — Evaluates the quality, strength, and completeness of evidence for a claim.
- [`/s4h-investigation-source-trace`](docs/investigation/source-trace.md) — Traces a claim back to its origin: who first made it, what evidence it rested on, and how it has been distorted in transmission.
- [`/s4h-investigation-triangulation`](docs/investigation/triangulation.md) — Verifies a claim across genuinely independent sources, distinguishing amplification from true corroboration.

#### `/s4h-economics`

- [`/s4h-economics`](docs/economics/README.md) — Entry point for the economics toolkit.
- [`/s4h-economics-opportunity-cost`](docs/economics/opportunity-cost.md) — Maps what you give up by choosing X — because every choice forecloses alternatives, and the invisible cost is often the decisive one.
- [`/s4h-economics-incentive-mapping`](docs/economics/incentive-mapping.md) — Maps who benefits, who pays, and what behaviors the incentive structure produces.
- [`/s4h-economics-externalities`](docs/economics/externalities.md) — Identifies costs and benefits not borne by the actor — and whether they should be internalized.
- [`/s4h-economics-margin`](docs/economics/margin.md) — Applies marginal thinking: the relevant decision is always the next unit, not the average, and sunk costs are irrelevant.
- [`/s4h-economics-coordination`](docs/economics/coordination.md) — Diagnoses collective action failures and public goods problems — why individually rational behavior produces collectively irrational outcomes.

#### `/s4h-information`

- [`/s4h-information`](docs/information/README.md) — Entry point for the information toolkit.
- [`/s4h-information-signal-noise`](docs/information/signal-noise.md) — Separates meaningful signal from irrelevant noise in any dataset, communication, or situation — identifying what carries actual information and what is static.
- [`/s4h-information-compression`](docs/information/compression.md) — Finds the shortest lossless representation of a message or model — the minimum description that preserves what matters.
- [`/s4h-information-redundancy`](docs/information/redundancy.md) — Identifies where redundancy is protective versus wasteful — and whether removing it creates brittleness or just bloat.
- [`/s4h-information-entropy`](docs/information/entropy.md) — Measures how much genuine uncertainty or surprise is in a source — and what that implies for decisions that depend on it.

#### `/s4h-cognition`

- [`/s4h-cognition`](docs/cognition/README.md) — Entry point for the cognition toolkit.
- [`/s4h-cognition-attention`](docs/cognition/attention.md) — Maps what is and isn't receiving cognitive attention in a situation — and whether the right things are being prioritised.
- [`/s4h-cognition-mental-models`](docs/cognition/mental-models.md) — Surfaces the mental models driving a decision or interpretation — and tests whether they fit the actual situation.
- [`/s4h-cognition-metacognition`](docs/cognition/metacognition.md) — Examines how you are thinking, not just what you are thinking — catching reasoning errors before they compound.
- [`/s4h-cognition-cognitive-load`](docs/cognition/cognitive-load.md) — Identifies where cognitive load is exceeding capacity — and redesigns the task, environment, or communication to work within limits.

### Think Differently

- [`/s4h-creativity-consider-factors`](docs/creativity/consider-factors.md) — Apply Edward de Bono's CAF (Consider All Factors) tool to map every relevant factor before making a decision or taking action.
- [`/s4h-creativity-lateral-thinking`](docs/creativity/lateral-thinking.md) — Apply Edward de Bono's lateral thinking to escape dominant patterns and generate genuinely new directions.

- [`/s4h-creativity-six-hats`](docs/creativity/six-hats.md) — Apply Edward de Bono's Six Thinking Hats for structured parallel thinking.
- [`/s4h-creativity-water-logic`](docs/creativity/water-logic.md) — Apply Edward de Bono's water logic for flow-based, non-judgmental exploration.

#### `/s4h-analogy`

- [`/s4h-analogy`](docs/analogy/README.md) — Entry point for the analogy toolkit.
- [`/s4h-analogy-boundary-testing`](docs/analogy/boundary-testing.md) — Finds where an analogy breaks down before it's relied upon.
- [`/s4h-analogy-domain-transfer`](docs/analogy/domain-transfer.md) — Imports solutions from unrelated domains by finding structural similarities between your problem and solved problems elsewhere.

#### `/s4h-play`

- [`/s4h-play`](docs/play/README.md) — Entry point for the play toolkit.
- [`/s4h-play-constraint-inversion`](docs/play/constraint-inversion.md) — Removes or inverts the main constraint to see what becomes possible — then uses those unconstrained solutions to find real ones.

#### `/s4h-evolution`

- [`/s4h-evolution-variation-selection`](docs/evolution/variation-selection.md) — Applies the variation-selection framework to any adaptive system — identifying what is varying, what selects, and what survives to replicate.

- [`/s4h-evolution-arms-race`](docs/evolution/arms-race.md) — Diagnoses co-evolutionary escalation dynamics — where two parties keep adapting to each other in a cycle with no stable end.

### Think About People

## Examples

#### The political landscape through game theory
The current political climate feels like everyone is acting against their own interests. `/s4h-game-theory-iterated` maps the repeated-game structure: parties face short-term defection incentives even when cooperation would benefit everyone long-run, and a shrinking shadow of the future (declining expected gains from future cooperation) explains why moderation keeps losing even when most people say they want it. The equilibrium is stable — not because of irrationality, but because of it.

#### Why social media makes discourse worse despite everyone wanting better
Platforms want healthier discourse. Users say they want it. It keeps getting worse. `/s4h-systems-feedback-mapping` finds the reinforcing loop: engagement-optimised feeds reward content that provokes reaction, which selects for users who engage with provocation, which trains the algorithm to serve more of it. The stated goal and the actual incentive structure point in opposite directions. No individual decision made it this way — the system produced it.

#### The ethics and game theory of automating 40 jobs
Your company is considering automating a function that currently employs 40 people. `/s4h-ethics-council` runs five frameworks and surfaces a genuine conflict: utilitarian logic favours the automation, Kantian reasoning flags that treating displaced workers as means rather than ends requires more than an efficiency argument. `/s4h-game-theory-mechanism-design` then helps design a transition that aligns incentives — rather than announcing a decision and managing the fallout.

#### Whether the current wave of nationalism is unprecedented
The political nationalism of the 2020s feels like something new. `/s4h-historical-cycle-detection` finds four structural analogues — late-19th-century nativist movements, 1930s economic retrenchment, post-colonial identity politics, 1970s stagflation politics — and maps where the current situation matches the pattern and where it doesn't. More useful than "this has happened before" or "this time is different."

#### A career pivot with a closing window
You're considering leaving a stable senior role for an early-stage company. The question feels like yes or no. `/s4h-strategy-positioning` maps how your options change if you wait two years versus going now. `/s4h-decision-reversibility-analysis` finds the move is partially reversible in the medium term — but with a specific window that closes as you get further from hands-on execution. The pairing surfaces a time constraint neither skill would have found alone.

#### Why a new pricing model keeps failing despite making economic sense
The economics look clear: the new pricing model captures more value, aligns incentives, and is objectively fairer. It keeps getting rejected. `/s4h-economics-incentive-mapping` finds the problem: the people who must approve it face short-term losses even though the organisation wins long-term. `/s4h-economics-coordination` then diagnoses the collective action failure — each approver is waiting for someone else to absorb the transition cost first.

#### What a startup's fundraising pitch is actually communicating
A founder's pitch deck is clear and well-structured. Investors keep passing without giving substantive reasons. `/s4h-linguistics-framing` finds the issue: the deck frames the company as solving a problem investors already think is solved, which pre-loads skepticism before a single number is scrutinised. The facts haven't changed — the frame has been working against the argument the whole time.

#### Why a platform grows explosively then stalls at the same threshold
A consumer app hit 100,000 users fast, then plateaued. Growth tactics aren't working. `/s4h-network-effects` diagnoses the type of network effect at play — local, not global — which means the platform reaches saturation within clusters long before it reaches market saturation. `/s4h-network-weak-ties` then identifies the bridge connections that could carry growth across cluster boundaries.

#### Whether a dominant tech company's position is actually durable
The company looks unassailable: massive market share, capital, talent. `/s4h-ecology-keystone-species` maps the dependencies — which other players rely on it and which the platform itself relies on — and finds two suppliers with quietly disproportionate leverage. `/s4h-ecology-succession` then models what the ecosystem looks like in the next developmental stage, and whether the current dominant player is positioned for it or optimised for conditions that are already passing.

#### Why a product keeps winning technically and losing commercially
Engineers ship a product that outperforms competitors on every measurable dimension. It keeps underperforming in the market. `/s4h-design-user-needs` finds the gap: the features being optimised are the ones engineers care about, not the ones that determine adoption decisions. `/s4h-cognition-mental-models` then surfaces the mental model users bring to the product — and shows the product is being built against a different model than the one users actually hold.

#### How a species analogy reveals the flaw in a market entry strategy
A company plans to enter a mature market by doing what the market leader does, but cheaper. `/s4h-evolution-fitness-landscape` maps the landscape: the incumbent occupies a local optimum that is genuinely hard to displace incrementally — competing on the same dimensions means fighting on the incumbent's terms. The analysis points toward a different peak entirely: a niche the incumbent has structurally abandoned.

#### The hidden cost in every information-rich environment
A team drowns in data but keeps making the same avoidable mistakes. `/s4h-information-signal-noise` finds that the most decision-relevant signals are buried in low-frequency, low-urgency reports that nobody reads — while high-frequency, high-urgency signals (email, Slack, dashboards) carry almost no information about the actual problem. The team isn't uninformed; it's overloaded with noise while the signal sits unread.
