# cognition-attention

> Map what's capturing your focus, what's depleting it, and build structural defences — not just willpower.

**Trigger:** `/s4h-cognition-attention`

---

## What it does

Treats attention as a finite resource subject to systematic capture and depletion, and designs structural defences rather than relying on willpower. Drawing on William James's foundational distinction between voluntary and involuntary attention, this skill maps the full demand landscape of a specific person or context — what is legitimately claiming focus, what is hijacking it through salience and emotional charge, and what is quietly draining it through background cognitive overhead. The output is a prioritised defence plan with concrete environmental and workflow changes.

---

## When to use

- "I can't focus — my attention keeps getting pulled away."
- Designing a deep work schedule or focus system
- You want to understand what's actually consuming your cognitive bandwidth
- Building a workflow or environment for someone else that needs to respect attention limits
- "I finish the day exhausted but I don't know what I actually did."

---

## Example

**Input:**
> I'm a product manager. I'm supposed to be doing strategic work — roadmap, prioritisation, writing specs — but I spend most of the day in Slack, triaging bugs reported in a shared channel, answering questions that come in via direct message, and getting pulled into meetings. By 4pm I'm too depleted to think strategically. I'm not lazy. I'm just always busy.

**Output:**

**Attention Demand Map**

| Source | Type | Estimated % of bandwidth | Legitimacy |
|---|---|---|---|
| Slack (shared bug channel) | Capture threat — high salience, continuous | ~30% | Low: reactive triage is not a PM responsibility |
| Direct message questions | Capture threat — social obligation, interpersonal | ~20% | Mixed: some legitimate, most deflectable |
| Unscheduled meetings | Legitimate demand + boundary failure | ~25% | Mixed: some required, most attendable async |
| Strategic work (roadmap, specs) | Legitimate demand | ~10% | High: the core job |
| Background anxiety (open loops, unread items) | Depletion source | ~15% | None: entirely eliminable |

**Core Problem:** Boundary failure and environment design failure. Strategic work is being outcompeted in real time by high-salience reactive demands that have no time boundary. The attention economy is rigged against deep work because there is no structural protection — everything competes in the same unfiltered stream.

**Defence Plan**

| Threat | Defence Type | Specific Action |
|---|---|---|
| Shared bug channel | Batching + Elimination | Leave the channel. Triage is an engineering or support function; get explicit with stakeholders that you will not monitor it. If you must, check once at noon only. |
| Direct message questions | Batching | Set a status: "I'm in focus mode until noon; DMs checked at 12 and 4." Most urgent things can wait 90 minutes. |
| Unscheduled meetings | Protection + Boundary | Block 9am–12pm daily as no-meeting time in your calendar. Accept no meeting requests during this window. |
| Slack ambient presence | Elimination | Quit Slack entirely during morning focus blocks. Phone in another room. Notifications off. |
| Open loops (unread items, undecided things) | Offloading | 10-minute end-of-day offload ritual: write every open loop and pending item into a trusted list. This clears background cognitive load so the brain stops running them as background processes overnight. |

**Priority Actions:**
1. Block 9–12 as protected focus time starting Monday — this is the single highest-leverage change
2. Leave or mute the bug channel — you are absorbing a responsibility that isn't yours
3. Implement the end-of-day offload ritual — background depletion is invisible but cumulative

**Protected Time Structure:**
- 9:00–12:00: Focus block — strategic work only, Slack closed, phone away
- 12:00–12:30: Communication triage (DMs, emails, bug channel if required)
- 12:30–5:00: Meetings, collaboration, reactive work
- 5:00–5:10: End-of-day offload — capture all open loops into a list before stopping

The goal is not to become unavailable. It is to ensure that the most cognitively demanding work happens when cognitive capacity is highest and the environment is structured to support it.
