# creativity-consider-factors

> Maps every relevant factor before deciding or acting — the pre-flight checklist for consequential decisions.

**Trigger:** `/creativity-consider-factors`

---

## What it does

Applies de Bono's CAF (Consider All Factors) tool: inventories every factor that could affect the outcome before committing. Unlike PMI (which evaluates an idea) or Six Hats (which applies multiple perspectives), CAF is a completeness check — its job is to make sure nothing important was overlooked.

---

## When to use

- About to take a significant action and want to make sure nothing is missed
- "Have I thought of everything?"
- Been surprised before by things that should have been anticipated
- Preparing for a decision that will be hard to reverse

## When *not* to use

- You need to evaluate the factors once you have them → [six-hats](six-hats.md) or [criteria-weighting](../decision/criteria-weighting.md)
- You need to generate options, not audit them → [alternatives](alternatives.md)

---

## Example

**Input:**
> We're about to announce a company restructure that moves three teams under a new VP. What factors should we consider before announcing?

**Output:**

**People factors**
- How will the individuals directly affected (3 team leads losing their direct VP relationship) feel, and what do they need to hear?
- Are there team members who've been vocal about wanting to stay in current structure?
- How does this affect compensation expectations, title, or career trajectory for anyone?
- Who in these teams might look for a new job if the change signals uncertainty?

**Communication factors**
- What's the order of notification? (Affected people must hear before anyone else)
- How much notice time between announcement and effective date?
- What questions will people ask that you're not prepared to answer?
- Is there anyone who will leak this before the announcement if they know early?

**Organizational factors**
- Does the new VP have the bandwidth and context to take on three new teams?
- Are there in-progress projects that span the old and new structure that need continuity plans?
- What happens to existing cross-functional rituals (standups, reviews) that may now have the wrong people in the room?

**Stakeholder factors**
- Do customers of these teams need to know, or will they be confused by the change in contacts?
- Are there vendor or partner relationships that are tied to specific team leads who are now moving?
- Does the board need to know, or does this fall under executive discretion?

**Timing factors**
- Is there anything in the next 30 days (performance reviews, planning cycles, hiring processes) that the restructure affects?
- Is this announcement landing during a high-stress period for any of these teams?

**Most commonly overlooked:**
The second-order people moves — who voluntarily leaves or disengages as a result, not immediately but over the following 60-90 days. Most restructure plans account for immediate reaction; few account for the slow-burn disengagement from people who weren't directly affected but felt their stability reduce.
