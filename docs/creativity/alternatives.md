# creativity-alternatives

> Generates all options before evaluating any — the antidote to false dichotomies.

**Trigger:** `/s4h-creativity-alternatives`

---

## What it does

Applies de Bono's APC (Alternatives, Possibilities, Choices) tool: sets a quota of 5+ options and prevents evaluation until the full inventory is complete. The first two options that come to mind are rarely the best — but they crowd out everything else when evaluation starts too early.

---

## When to use

- About to choose between two options (false dichotomy likely)
- "What are all my choices here?"
- Planning something and want to ensure no approach was missed
- About to go with the first good idea

## When *not* to use

- You need to see options at multiple levels of abstraction → [concept-fan](concept-fan.md)
- You need to evaluate options once they're visible → [criteria-weighting](../decision/criteria-weighting.md)

---

## Example

**Input:**
> We have an underperforming team member who's been with the company 3 years. Performance has declined over the last 6 months. We're deciding between a performance improvement plan and letting them go. What are our options?

**Output:**

*Setting the quota: finding at least 6 options before evaluating any.*

**Option 1: Performance Improvement Plan (PIP)**
Structured 60-90 day plan with clear metrics, regular check-ins, and defined consequences.

**Option 2: Termination**
Exit the employee now with appropriate severance.

**Option 3: Role change within the team**
Move them to a role that better fits their current strengths. Decline in this role doesn't necessarily indicate decline across all roles.

**Option 4: Leave of absence**
Offer a paid or unpaid leave — something personal may be affecting performance that a leave could resolve.

**Option 5: Explicit conversation before any formal process**
Neither PIP nor exit — a direct conversation about what's changed and what they want, with no formal status. Some performance declines resolve themselves once the conversation happens.

**Option 6: Reduce scope, keep the person**
Move them to a smaller or more contained role (individual contributor vs. lead, for example). Retains institutional knowledge at a level that matches current output.

**Option 7: Peer mentoring or coaching**
Pair them with a strong performer for 60 days before deciding anything. Addresses whether the issue is skill gap vs. motivation vs. something else.

**Option 8: Investigate the context first**
The decline started 6 months ago — did anything else change then? Team composition, manager, project type, personal circumstances? Understanding the cause changes which option is appropriate.

---

**Now evaluating:**
The binary (PIP vs. exit) bypasses Options 5, 8, and 3, any of which might be more appropriate depending on the cause of the decline. Option 8 is the prerequisite — understanding why performance declined in the last 6 months determines which of the other options is actually the right one.
