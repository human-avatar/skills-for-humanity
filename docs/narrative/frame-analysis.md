# narrative-frame-analysis

> Identifies the current frame around a situation and generates alternative frames that reveal different truths.

**Trigger:** `/s4h-narrative-frame-analysis`

---

## What it does

Makes the current implicit frame explicit, generates 3-5 alternative frames, and identifies which frame best serves the communication goal — not by distorting truth, but by revealing the aspect of truth that needs to be seen.

---

## When to use

- "The framing of this is wrong"
- People keep interpreting a situation in a way that blocks the outcome you need
- "Change the narrative"
- Same facts, different meaning needed

---

## Example

**Input:**
> We missed our Q3 revenue target by 18%. The board will see this as failure. Is there a different frame?

**Output:**

**Current implicit frame:**

"We promised X, we delivered Y, the gap is failure." This is the measurement frame — it treats the target as the ground truth and reality as deviation from it.

**Alternative frames:**

**Frame 1 — The learning frame**
"We ran an aggressive experiment in Q3 that surfaced three important things: our enterprise sales cycle is 40% longer than our SMB cycle, our pricing needs adjustment for the mid-market, and our sales capacity was sized for a conversion rate we hadn't validated. We now have the data to size Q4 correctly."
*What it reveals:* The miss was informative, not just negative.

**Frame 2 — The market signal frame**
"Revenue was 18% below target, but three competitors also missed guidance this quarter in this segment. This is a market signal, not an execution signal."
*What it reveals:* The miss may have an external cause that the target didn't account for.

**Frame 3 — The foundation frame**
"We took our Q3 shortfall and made the structural investments that would have prevented it — [specific hires, product changes, process changes]. These weren't possible to make while chasing the target."
*What it reveals:* The miss created space for something.

**Frame 4 — The trendline frame**
"Month-over-month growth accelerated every month in Q3. We missed the Q3 aggregate target, but the velocity at end of Q3 sets Q4 up to perform."
*Only valid if the underlying data supports it.*

---

**Caution:** A frame is only ethical if it's true. These frames aren't spins on a failure — they're different lenses on the same reality. If the business is genuinely in trouble, the right frame is honesty, not repositioning. But if the miss was informative, external, or accompanied by real progress, those things deserve to be part of the story.

**Best frame for a board audience:** Frame 1 (learning) + Frame 3 (foundation), if both are true. Boards generally respond better to "we learned something and changed course" than to "the target was wrong."
