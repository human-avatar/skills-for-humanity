# narrative-tension-mapping

> Finds or creates the tension that makes communication worth paying attention to.

**Trigger:** `/narrative-tension-mapping`

---

## What it does

Identifies the genuine tension in a situation — the gap between where things are and where they should be, the unresolved question, the stakes that make it matter. Surfaces how to make that tension visible in the communication so the audience has a reason to pay attention.

---

## When to use

- "This feels flat"
- Communication is technically correct but no one engages
- "Make this compelling"
- Presentation or proposal that isn't creating urgency

---

## Example

**Input:**
> I'm presenting our annual product roadmap. It lists features by quarter, has nice visuals, and explains each feature. But previous roadmap presentations have felt dry and forgettable. Find the tension.

**Output:**

**The current structure:**
Feature → Quarter → Description → Next feature. This is an inventory, not a story. There's no tension because there's no question the audience needs answered.

**Finding the genuine tension:**

A roadmap presentation has inherent tension that's almost never surfaced: we're betting on specific things to build in a world where we can't build everything, while competitors are building different things, while customers are asking for different things still. Every roadmap is a collection of high-stakes choices. The drama is in the choices, not the list.

**Three tensions worth surfacing:**

**Tension 1 — Scarcity:** "We have 40 things on our backlog and we're building 8. Here are the 8, and here's why we chose them over the other 32." This makes the audience feel the trade-off. They understand what you're NOT building and why, which makes the choices feel deliberate rather than arbitrary.

**Tension 2 — Risk:** "These are the three bets where we're most uncertain, and here's what would make us confident we got them right." Roadmaps presented as certainties feel dishonest and boring. Roadmaps that acknowledge strategic uncertainty feel real and create genuine engagement.

**Tension 3 — Competition:** "Our competitors are moving in X direction. We're consciously moving in Y direction. Here's why that's the right bet." This makes the roadmap feel like a strategic decision, not a feature list.

**How to apply:**

Open the presentation with the question the roadmap is an answer to: "With limited capacity, in a competitive market, how do we build the thing that most strengthens our position?" Then show that each roadmap section is an answer to a specific version of that question. Close with what you're watching to know if you got it right.

The tension transforms the audience from passive receivers of information into active evaluators of strategy — which is what you actually want them to be.
