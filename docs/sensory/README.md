# 👁️ Sensory

> *The most important information is often present but not being registered.*

---

## What this category is

Sensory tools apply discipline to observation — the act of actually seeing what's there before deciding what it means. Most thinking errors happen not in the reasoning but in the observation: we rush to interpret before we've fully observed, we filter out details that don't match our existing frame, and we confuse noise for signal.

These tools slow the process down at the right point. Observe before interpreting. Separate what's actually there from what you expect to find.

---

## When to reach for it

- Something is happening and you're not sure you're seeing it clearly
- Overwhelmed with data or information and not sure what matters
- Suspicion that important details are being missed or dismissed
- Tendency to jump to interpretation before fully observing
- Complex situation that needs careful reading before action
- Want to challenge your own existing frame by looking more rigorously at the actual evidence

---

## When *not* to reach for it

**Don't use sensory tools as a substitute for analysis.** Disciplined observation is the input to good analysis — it's not the analysis itself. After observing carefully, move to the appropriate analytical tool.

**Don't use these tools when the problem is a judgment problem, not an observation problem.** If you've observed the situation clearly but don't know what to do about it, that's a [Decision](../decision/), [Logic](../logic/), or [Ethics](../ethics/) question.

The nearest neighbor: [Aesthetic](../aesthetic/) — which analyzes the formal properties of an artifact. Sensory tools are about the quality of observation before any analysis.
