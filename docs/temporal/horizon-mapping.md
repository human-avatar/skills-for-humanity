# temporal-horizon-mapping

> Maps the consequences of a decision across short, medium, and long time horizons simultaneously.

**Trigger:** `/s4h-temporal-horizon-mapping`

---

## What it does

Traces the implications of a decision across three horizons — short (1-3 months), medium (1-2 years), and long (5-10 years). Identifies short-term gains that create long-term lock-in, long-term investments that require short-term courage, and decisions that look different depending on the time scale you're holding.

---

## When to use

- A decision looks good now but you're worried about long-term consequences
- Short-term and long-term pressures are in conflict
- "What does this look like in 5 years?"
- About to make a commitment and want to understand the full horizon

---

## Example

**Input:**
> We're considering licensing our technology to a large strategic partner instead of building our own distribution. It gives us immediate revenue and reach. Map the horizons.

**Output:**

**The decision:** License technology to a large partner for distribution rather than building direct distribution.

---

**Short-term (1-3 months)**
- ✅ Immediate revenue: licensing fees provide cash flow without the overhead of building distribution
- ✅ Reach: partner's existing customer base provides access that would take 12+ months to build
- ✅ Validation: a large partner choosing your technology is a meaningful signal
- ⚠️ Negotiation overhead: deal terms for strategic licensing are complex and time-consuming
- ⚠️ Resource diversion: deal management diverts attention from product development

**Medium-term (1-2 years)**
- ✅ Revenue scales with partner's distribution — if they grow, you grow passively
- ✅ Market insights from partner's customer feedback
- ⚠️ Loss of direct customer relationships: you know the partner, not their customers
- ⚠️ Dependency risk: if partner's priorities shift, your revenue is at risk
- ⚠️ Partner now knows your roadmap: they can build your capabilities themselves
- ⚠️ Your own distribution muscle atrophies — you've stopped developing it

**Long-term (5-10 years)**
- 🚨 You may have created your acquirer or your competitor: a partner with deep access to your technology and your customer base has everything needed to replicate or buy you
- 🚨 Captive dependency: if the partner becomes 60%+ of revenue, you have no ability to walk away from unfavorable renegotiations
- ⚠️ Brand invisibility: your technology powers the partner's product, not your own; you remain unknown to end customers
- 💡 If managed well (IP protections, customer co-access, non-exclusivity): the revenue base may fund the direct distribution you couldn't build on your own

**The critical insight across horizons:**

The deal that looks like expansion at 3 months looks like dependency at 2 years and potentially like a strategic trap at 5 years. The variable that determines which outcome materializes: whether the deal preserves your ability to build independent distribution, access end customers, and own the relationship.

**Recommendation:**
Pursue the deal with these conditions: non-exclusive license, co-branding or customer co-access clause, IP protections that prevent partner reverse-engineering, and a defined exit that preserves your ability to build direct distribution. Without those terms, the short-term gain creates long-term structural weakness.
