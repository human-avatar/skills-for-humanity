# temporal-futures-mapping

> Explores possible, probable, and preferable futures using scenario thinking.

**Trigger:** `/temporal-futures-mapping`

---

## What it does

Applies futures cone methodology: maps Possible futures (full range), Plausible futures (given current trends), Probable futures (most likely trajectory), and Preferable futures (what you want to make happen). Builds three scenarios — continuation, disruption, and transformation — and identifies what decisions made today most influence which future materializes.

---

## When to use

- Strategic planning for an uncertain environment
- "How might this play out?"
- Scenarios thinking for investment, hiring, or roadmap decisions
- "What are we planning for and what are we ignoring?"

---

## Example

**Input:**
> We're a mid-size media company. How might the next 5 years play out for us?

**Output:**

**Key uncertainties (what drives divergence between futures):**
1. Speed and depth of AI content generation adoption
2. Whether platform aggregators (social, search) continue to commoditize distribution
3. Whether audiences pay directly for media or remain ad-funded
4. Regulatory environment for AI-generated content

---

**Scenario 1 — Continuation**
*Current trends continue at roughly current pace.*

AI supplements editorial workflows but doesn't replace them. Ad revenue continues slow structural decline. Subscription growth continues for outlets with strong brand and loyalty. The middle tier (not niche enough to have loyal subscribers, not large enough to have advertising scale) continues to struggle. Your company's outcome depends heavily on whether you're building toward subscriptions or stuck in ad dependency.

**Scenario 2 — Disruption**
*One or more current trends accelerate sharply.*

AI-generated content floods the internet within 18 months. Commodity content (news recaps, summaries, basic explainers) becomes essentially free and infinite. Distribution platforms optimize for AI-generated content because it's cheaper. Human-written content without clear differentiation loses its market. Companies that survive are those with: strong audience relationships, unique perspectives (not just unique information), events/experiences, or proprietary data. Everyone else consolidates or disappears.

**Scenario 3 — Transformation**
*The fundamental model of media changes.*

Audiences, overwhelmed by AI-generated content, move toward curation and trusted relationships rather than content volume. The scarcity shifts from content to trust. New models emerge: creator-led subscription communities, AI-assisted but human-curated experiences, deep niche coverage at scale. The winner isn't the company with the most content — it's the company with the deepest relationship.

---

**Preferable future:**
A version of Scenario 3 — where your brand becomes a trusted curation and relationship layer, not a content production machine.

**What decisions today most influence the outcome:**
1. Subscription vs. ad revenue mix — shifting toward subscriptions now reduces exposure to AI-generated content commoditization
2. Brand investment — your editorial voice is the moat in all three scenarios
3. AI integration strategy — using AI to do more of what you do, rather than replacing what you do, positions you for the disruption scenario
4. Audience relationships — the community and direct relationship infrastructure you build in the next 12 months is the survival asset in the worst-case scenario

**What to watch for:**
If platform-driven traffic drops more than 20% in the next 6 months, the disruption scenario is arriving faster than expected. That's the signal to accelerate the subscription and brand investments.
