# ⏳ Temporal

> *The same action is correct in one phase and disastrous in another.*

---

## What this category is

Temporal tools apply structured thinking to the time dimension of any situation — what consequences a decision has at different horizons, what cycle the situation is an instance of, what futures are plausible and preferable, and whether now is actually the right time to act. These tools exist because short-term and long-term thinking are genuinely different modes, and most decisions are made primarily in one of them when both are needed.

The core discipline: map consequences across time before committing to action. Short-term gains that create long-term lock-in are everywhere. Long-term investments that require short-term courage are underweighted constantly.

---

## When to reach for it

- A decision looks different at different time scales and you need to hold them simultaneously
- Wondering whether to act now or wait — and not sure the instinct is right
- A situation has a familiar cycle quality — want to know where in the pattern you are
- Making strategic plans that need to account for multiple possible futures
- About to make a long-term commitment and want to map the horizon
- Timing feels uncertain — market timing, organizational timing, personal timing

---

## When *not* to reach for it

**Don't use temporal tools when the timing is genuinely irrelevant.** Some decisions are time-invariant — the right answer doesn't change based on when you make it. Save the temporal toolkit for decisions where timing matters.

**Don't confuse temporal analysis with futures prediction.** Futures mapping and cycle detection improve judgment about what's likely — they don't predict. Over-confidence in temporal analysis is a known failure mode.

The nearest neighbor: [Historical](../historical/) — which looks backward at what has happened, while temporal tools look forward at what might happen and sideways at the current moment in a cycle.
