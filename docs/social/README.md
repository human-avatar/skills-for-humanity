# 🌐 Social

> *Follow the incentives. The behavior makes sense when you find them.*

---

## What this category is

Social tools apply structured analysis to organizations, groups, and political situations — mapping who holds power (formal and informal), what incentives are actually driving behavior, how group dynamics shape what's said and decided, and how to build the coalition a proposal needs to succeed. These tools work on the collective dimension of human situations, where individual motivations combine into patterns that are often counterintuitive.

The central insight: organizations behave the way they're incentivized to behave, not the way they intend to behave. Finding the real incentive structure explains most organizational dysfunction.

---

## When to reach for it

- A proposal needs buy-in from multiple stakeholders and you need to build alignment
- A team is behaving in ways that are hard to understand or predict
- A decision keeps getting made "wrong" despite everyone agreeing it should be made "right"
- You need to understand who actually decides something — not who officially decides
- An initiative is failing despite good intentions and genuine effort
- You need to navigate organizational politics on something important

---

## When *not* to reach for it

**Don't use social tools as a substitute for leadership.** Mapping the power dynamics doesn't tell you what to do with that information — that requires judgment. These tools inform action; they don't replace it.

**Don't use these tools to manipulate rather than align.** Understanding incentive structures should help you find genuine alignment, not exploit levers. If the goal is manipulation rather than alignment, stop and use [Ethics](../ethics/).

The nearest neighbor: [Emotional](../emotional/) — which focuses on individuals and relationships. Social tools focus on groups, organizations, and structural power.
