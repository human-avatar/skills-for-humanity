# communication-objection-mapping

> Maps likely objections before you deliver a proposal — so nothing arrives as a surprise.

**Trigger:** `/communication-objection-mapping`

---

## What it does

Identifies all stakeholders, surfaces their likely objections and the underlying concerns behind them, assesses each objection (addressable, frameable, or unresolvable), and produces a preparation plan. Objections anticipated feel addressed; objections that land as surprises derail.

---

## When to use

- About to present something contentious
- "What will they push back on?"
- Preparing for a difficult meeting
- Want to steelman the opposition before presenting

---

## Example

**Input:**
> I'm proposing we stop accepting new enterprise deals and focus exclusively on our SMB segment for 6 months. Map the objections.

**Output:**

**Stakeholder: Head of Sales**
*Surface objection:* "We're leaving money on the table — enterprise deals are our biggest contracts."
*Underlying concern:* Her quota is built on enterprise ARR. This proposal changes the target she's being measured against. She may also worry this is a signal that her team's skills (enterprise selling) are being devalued.
*Type:* Addressable — needs quota adjustment and explicit acknowledgment of team value during the transition.

**Stakeholder: CEO/Board**
*Surface objection:* "Our investors expect us to demonstrate enterprise traction."
*Underlying concern:* Enterprise contracts signal credibility to enterprise investors and future buyers. SMB focus may look like a retreat from the more prestigious market.
*Type:* Addressable by framing — position this as "we're building a repeatable SMB motion that enterprise buyers will value" rather than "we're giving up on enterprise."

**Stakeholder: Customer Success**
*Surface objection:* "Our biggest accounts need enterprise support features we haven't built."
*Underlying concern:* If we deprioritize enterprise, existing enterprise customers get less investment. CS is worried about churn from current enterprise accounts.
*Type:* Requires clarification — the proposal is "no new enterprise deals," not "deprioritize existing enterprise." This objection needs to be addressed in the proposal itself.

**Stakeholder: Engineering**
*Surface objection:* Probably supportive — enterprise deals create custom work.
*Underlying concern:* None likely. Engineering may actually welcome this.
*Type:* Potential ally, not objector.

**Unresolvable objection:**
If the company is in a fundraising process and investors specifically want enterprise ARR, this proposal may be timing-incompatible. No framing resolves an actual investor requirement.

**Preparation:**
1. Fix the proposal first: explicitly state that existing enterprise customers are not affected
2. Prepare a quota restructuring proposal for Sales before the meeting — she can't support this without knowing how her team is measured
3. Brief the CEO 1:1 before the group meeting to align on the investor narrative
