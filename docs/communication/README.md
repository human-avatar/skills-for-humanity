# 💬 Communication

> *Communication fails at the receiver, not the sender.*

---

## What this category is

Communication tools address the space between what you intend to say and what your audience actually receives. They work on the four failure modes of communication: not understanding your audience, sending a message that can be misread, choosing the wrong channel for the content, and being blindsided by objections you didn't anticipate.

These tools are pre-communication — they run before you send, present, or publish. The goal is to close the gap between intent and impact before it costs you.

---

## When to reach for it

- About to deliver something important — proposal, presentation, difficult message
- A previous communication didn't land the way you expected
- You're not sure your audience will understand or respond the way you need
- You're preparing for a potentially contentious conversation
- You're choosing between channels (meeting vs. email, async vs. sync, document vs. conversation)
- Something you've written feels right to you but you're not confident it reads right to others

---

## When *not* to reach for it

**Don't confuse communication problems with content problems.** If the substance of what you're saying is wrong, better communication of it won't help. Fix the content first.

**Don't over-engineer low-stakes messages.** Not every email needs audience modeling. These tools are for communications where the stakes of misunderstanding are real.

The nearest neighbor: [Narrative](../narrative/) — which focuses on the story structure and framing of communication, while this category focuses on audience fit and message clarity.
