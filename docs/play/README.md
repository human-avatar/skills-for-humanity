# 🎲 Play

> *The worst possible version is usually the clearest map to the best version.*

---

## What this category is

Play tools use deliberate unconventionality to access ideas that conventional thinking misses. Not because playfulness is fun (though it is), but because fixed mental positions block the sight lines to non-obvious solutions. The tools in this category work by forcibly inhabiting a different position: removing constraints, reversing perspectives, introducing randomness, or designing failure deliberately.

This is serious methodology: some of the most durable design insights emerge from inverting the brief, and some of the worst blind spots dissolve the moment you genuinely inhabit the opposing perspective.

---

## When to reach for it

- Conventional brainstorming keeps producing safe, predictable results
- Constraint feels like the ceiling on all thinking
- Missing what the other side (competitor, critic, user, adversary) sees
- Stuck in the same circles; need stimulus from completely outside the problem
- A team is being too polite to say what they actually think about a direction

---

## When *not* to reach for it

**Play tools are entry points, not endpoints.** They generate raw material — unconventional directions, inverted assumptions, fresh angles. The output needs to be evaluated, not acted on directly. Use [Creativity](../creativity/) tools like PMI or Six Hats after play generates the raw ideas.

**Don't use play when precision is what's needed.** If a calculation is wrong or an argument has a logical flaw, play won't fix it — [Logic](../logic/) will.

The nearest neighbor: [Creativity](../creativity/) — which uses the structured de Bono toolkit to generate and evaluate ideas. Play is for when you need to break out of the frame that the structured toolkit is operating within.
