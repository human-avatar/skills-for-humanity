# 🔬 Logic

> *Broken reasoning produces confident wrong answers. The fix isn't more thinking — it's better structure.*

---

## What this category is

Logic tools validate, interrogate, and repair reasoning. They cover the full spectrum from a quick argument check to a five-advisor council with peer review. These tools don't tell you what to believe; they tell you whether your reasoning for believing it actually holds.

The toolkit handles distinct types of logical work: argument validation (does the conclusion follow?), consistency checking (does the document contradict itself?), causality mapping (what actually causes what?), constraint mapping (what's actually fixed?), and fixing broken reasoning rather than just diagnosing it.

---

## When to reach for it

- An argument, proposal, or plan needs to withstand scrutiny before being acted on
- You've invested in a position and want it stress-tested by something other than your own reasoning
- A document or spec has grown incrementally and may have drifted into internal contradiction
- You need to understand what breaks if X changes — root cause or dependency analysis
- You have broken reasoning and need it repaired, not just diagnosed
- A complex decision rests on a chain of logic that needs to be sound end-to-end

---

## When *not* to reach for it

**Don't use logic tools when the real problem is missing information.** You can't logic your way to truth from false premises. Verify the facts first.

**Don't confuse logical validity with practical wisdom.** A sound argument proves a conclusion follows from premises — it doesn't prove the premises are complete, or that the right question is being asked. Ethics, identity, and systems tools address dimensions logic doesn't.

The nearest neighbor: [Probability](../probability/) — which handles reasoning under uncertainty with quantified estimates, while logic tools handle the structure of arguments and inferences.
