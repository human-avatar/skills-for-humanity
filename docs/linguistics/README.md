# 🗣️ Linguistics

> *You cannot escape framing. You can only choose it deliberately.*

---

## What this category is

Linguistics tools examine language as a system of meaning — not just what words say, but what they frame, imply, carry, and do over time. They exist because language is not a neutral medium. Every word choice activates associations, every sentence structure presupposes a world, and every description of a situation is simultaneously an argument about it. The question is not whether your language is doing this work — it always is — but whether it is doing the work you intend.

The intellectual lineage runs from Wittgenstein's insight that meaning is use (not reference), through Grice's discovery that speakers communicate far more than they literally say, to Austin and Searle's speech act theory (utterances do not just describe the world; they act on it), to Lakoff's framing research (cognitive frames determine what solutions are thinkable), and Pinker's work on language, connotation, and the limits of euphemism. These are not academic curiosities — they are the operating mechanisms of every political campaign, every product launch, every difficult conversation, and every high-stakes negotiation.

These tools run before and during communication: they surface what your current language is doing, expose what is being implied or presupposed, and equip you to make deliberate choices rather than inherit the language of whatever frame you first encountered.

---

## When to reach for it

- A conversation keeps hitting the same wall, no matter how many times you address the substance
- A word or phrase is generating reactions you did not intend
- You suspect you are accepting terms that are working against you
- A key term means something different to different audiences in the same conversation
- An argument seems reasonable on its face but something is wrong underneath
- A word you rely on is being contested, captured, or changed
- You are about to communicate something important and want to audit your language before sending

---

## When *not* to reach for it

**Don't use linguistics tools when the problem is content, not language.** If the position is wrong, better framing of it will not help — and may make things worse by obscuring the substantive problem. Fix the content first.

**Linguistics is not spin.** These tools are for making language deliberate, not for making bad things sound good. The audit is diagnostic: if your language is working against your intent, you need to know. If your intent itself is the problem, these tools will surface that too.

The nearest neighbor: [Communication](../communication/) — which focuses on message clarity, audience modeling, and channel selection. Use communication tools when you know what you want to say and need to say it better. Use linguistics tools when you're not sure what your language is already saying.
